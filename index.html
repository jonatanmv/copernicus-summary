Copernicus Project Summary and References

Since 24 January 2023 a new Copernicus Data Space Ecosystem has been launched to provide access to all Sentinel data with new features for visualisation and data processing.

Copernicus provide knowledge, but all start with DATA. The adopted policy under this project is based on a Full, Free and Open access to all that data.

Copernicus Governance

  • The European Commission manages the programme as manager
  • Technical coordination by ESA
  • And there is more actors like EUMESAT, ECMWF, EU agencies and Mercator Ocean.

Infraestructure

  • Sentinel 1A & 1B: Radar imagery, polar orbit, all weather, day and night
  • Sentinel 2A & 2B: High resolution optical imaging, polar orbit.
  • Sentinel 3A & 3B: Optical and altimetric sensors for marine and terrestrial parameters.
  • Sentinel 4: first launch in 2022. Atmospheric chemical monitoring
  • Sentinel 5p: to reduce data gaps between envisage an S-5
  • Sentinel 5: first launch in 2023. Atmospheric chemical monitoring in MetOp 2nsGen

Copernicus Satellite Data Sources

  • Managed by ESA (European Space Agency).
    • Open Access Hub (SCI Hub). The Copernicus Open Access Hub provides complete, free and open access to Sentinel data products....
      https://scihub.copernicus.eu

      https://spacedata.copernicus.eu
  • Managed by EUMETSAT (European Organisation for the Exploitation of Meteorological Satellites).
    • EUMETCast. Service is mostly Free and Open.
      https://www.eumetsat.int/eumetcast

In-Situ data

  • Observational data from ground, sea or airborne sensors used as auxiliary data, licensed for use in Copernicus
  • Use: Validation, Calibration, Information services
  • Implementation: Adapted to each Copernicus service and transversal coordination between services and EEA (European Environment Agency)
